Christian Nationalist Policy Framework Links Domestic Agenda to Iran Conflict Strategy

Defense Secretary Pete Hegseth and Heritage Foundation personnel are simultaneously advancing Christian nationalist policy implementation domestically and shaping military strategy toward Iran.

The Trump administration has advanced approximately 50% of Project 2025's 920-page policy agenda, according to tracking by The 19th News, PBS NewsHour, and Baptist News Global. The implemented measures include dismantling DEI programs, restricting LGBTQ protections, and redefining family structure through HHS directives. Heritage Foundation official Kevin Roberts has confirmed the Christian nationalist framework underlying these policies.

The same policy infrastructure is documented as influencing foreign military decisions. Investigative reporting from The Intercept identifies Defense Secretary Pete Hegseth's Christian nationalist theology as directly shaping Trump administration strategy toward Iran, with apocalyptic theological framing applied to Middle East conflict analysis. Hegseth has publicly stated religious motivations for military positions. The timing of these theological applications coincides with the February 28 initiation of US-Israel military operations against Iran—designated "Operation Epic Fury" by defense analysts.

Heritage Foundation personnel including Russell Vought and Kevin Roberts have confirmed the export of this Christian nationalist framework internationally through the Geneva Consensus Declaration network, now spanning 40 countries including several autocracies. Documentation shows the administration has withdrawn from 66 international organizations citing DEI and gender equity mandates as justification. USAID has been substantially dismantled, with remaining operations conditioned on alignment with Geneva Consensus principles.

The Iran conflict itself has escalated significantly. As of March 3, approximately 2,000 documented strikes have occurred, with 787 confirmed Iranian fatalities including 175 civilians from a single school strike, according to Crisis24 and Indrastra Global analysis. Supreme Leader Khamenei was killed in the initial operations. The Houthis have entered active military engagement, blockading Bab al-Mandab; the Strait of Hormuz is effectively closed. CSIS, the Atlantic Council, and CFR assessments document severe disruption to global energy and supply chains.

What remains unconfirmed: whether the theological framing applied to Iran strategy represents a deliberate policy doctrine or reflects individual personnel perspectives. No documented policy memo, formal interagency directive, or Defense Department guidance has been made public establishing Christian nationalist theology as official military decision-making framework. The precise degree of Hegseth's operational authority over Iran campaign decisions versus advisory capacity remains undocumented in public sources.
